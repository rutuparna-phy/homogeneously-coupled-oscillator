Physics of Homogeneously Coupled Oscillators (Equal Masses)

Normal Modes:

Symmetric Mode: Both masses oscillate in phase
Frequency: ω₀ (m₁ = m₂)

Antisymmetric Mode: Masses oscillate out of phase
Frequency: √(ω₀² + 2κ/m) where m₁ = m₂ = m

Beat Phenomenon:

Beat Frequency: Δω = √(ω₀² + 2κ/m) - ω₀

Energy Exchange Period: T = 2π/Δω

Energy oscillates completely between equal masses

Key Observation: With equal masses (m₁ = m₂), the system exhibits perfect energy transfer. When one oscillator starts with maximum amplitude and the other at rest, energy completely transfers back and forth - a hallmark of symmetric coupled oscillator systems.
